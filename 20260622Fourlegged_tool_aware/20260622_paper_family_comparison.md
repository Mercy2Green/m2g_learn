# Paper-Family Comparison for Tool-Aware Quadruped-Arm Mobile Manipulation

This document compares the papers and projects named in `20260622_base_information_for_research.md`. The organizing question is not simply what each work proposes, but how a limitation in one line of work becomes the motivation for the next.

## Family-level limitation chain

This family progresses from scalable action representation to heterogeneous-data and cross-embodiment transfer. FAST removes an action-token bottleneck; π0.5, GR00T N1, Gemini Robotics, and X-VLA then address data, reasoning, and embodiment mismatch, while VLM4VLA shows that a stronger generic VLM alone is insufficient. The recurring gap is that “embodiment” is represented at robot level, whereas a grasped tool dynamically changes the effective embodiment, collision geometry, reach, and wrench transmission.

## Family-level limitation chain

MoManipVLA turns fixed-base waypoints into feasible base-arm motion, while SG-VLA learns richer spatial state and WholeBodyVLA learns manipulation-aware locomotion. MobileVLA-R1 and MindExplore add explicit reasoning or memory for longer tasks. None jointly represents tool tip, grasp, collision envelope, interaction wrench, and support posture, leaving base-arm-tool feasibility as a distinct unsolved layer for a quadruped-arm platform.

## Family-level limitation chain

AffordGrasp and Aff-Grasp move from object identity to task-conditioned functional regions, but remain primarily visual/geometric. iTuP directly addresses the resulting failure: a semantically correct grasp may be mechanically unstable under the intended trajectory. Collision-aware and tool-design works further show that a tool changes occupied volume and even the feasible action space, yet none closes the loop with quadruped stance and base-arm reachability.

## Family-level limitation chain

Video Policy addresses data scarcity with predictive pixels; MoWM then repairs the pixel-versus-latent tradeoff, and OA-WAM repairs loss of object identity. WLA and GigaBrain add semantic decomposition or RL-based future anticipation, while MotionWAM addresses execution rate for whole-body control. The remaining issue is physical sufficiency: a plausible future frame or object slot does not establish that a tool contact is wrench-feasible, non-slipping, collision-free, and stable for a quadruped base.

## Family-level limitation chain

VLABench and RoboCerebra expose long-horizon reasoning failures; ManipBench and PhysToolBench narrow evaluation to low-level physical or tool understanding. Robo2VLM and RoboTwin 2.0 respond by scaling embodiment-relevant supervision and synthetic task data. The persistent gap is an executable benchmark where quadruped posture, base-arm reachability, functional grasp, tool collision, and contact success are measured together rather than through QA or tabletop completion alone.

## Family-level limitation chain

CV methods contribute precise masks and video priors, while LLM methods contribute open-vocabulary decomposition and retrieval. Robo2VLM closes part of the domain gap by converting robot trajectories into reasoning supervision. These mechanisms are transferable components, not sufficient solutions: they must be grounded by a robot-specific feasibility layer that converts semantic/visual tool knowledge into stable base-arm-tool execution.

## Cross-Family Takeaways

- Generalist VLAs are strong policy baselines, but their cross-embodiment mechanisms treat embodiment as fixed rather than changing after a tool is grasped.
- Mobile-manipulation work addresses base-arm or whole-body coordination, yet generally stops at object manipulation and does not couple stance with tool-tip contact.
- Affordance methods identify task-relevant grasp and functional regions, but visual affordance is not equivalent to wrench-feasible execution.
- iTuP provides the clearest direct evidence that correct tool and trajectory choices can still fail because of grasp-level force and torque transfer.
- Tool-aware collision avoidance shows that a held tool must alter the robot collision model, not remain a semantic object label.
- WAMs improve future anticipation, object identity, or data efficiency, but visual plausibility is not evidence of contact feasibility.
- Existing benchmarks separately test language, long-horizon reasoning, low-level manipulation, or physical tool QA; none jointly evaluates mobile whole-body tool execution.
- For a Go2+Piper-scale project, an explicit feasibility module is more reproducible and falsifiable than training a new large foundation model or video world model from scratch.

## Open Gaps for Our Research

- Tools are commonly represented as semantic categories, affordance masks, or grasp targets rather than force-transmitting extensions of the robot.
- Mobile-manipulation planners rarely optimize base pose, arm configuration, grasp, tool-tip path, and quadruped support stability in one objective.
- Existing VLAs generally lack explicit contact force, torque, slip, lever arm, and tool-axis variables.
- Affordance localization does not determine whether the selected functional part can be reached with a stable grasp and collision-free whole-body posture.
- A grasped tool changes the effective end effector and collision envelope, but most VLA state/action representations remain unchanged.
- WAM methods are unjustified overhead unless they predict task-relevant contact consequences better than a compact analytical or learned feasibility model.
- Tool benchmarks test physical reasoning in QA, while manipulation benchmarks rarely test closed-loop quadruped-arm tool use.
- Quadruped embodiment introduces support polygon, body attitude, foot contact, vibration, and arm-reaction constraints absent from fixed-base tool-use studies.

## Suggested Research Positioning

The smallest defensible gap is not “a tool-aware VLA for a quadruped,” but a demonstrated failure of ordinary mobile manipulation when a grasped tool changes execution feasibility. A focused paper could define a compact tool state—functional region, grasp frame, tool axis/tip, collision envelope, and expected interaction wrench—and use it to score or optimize coupled base posture, arm trajectory, and grasp. The claim should be limited to showing that this representation corrects systematic failures on reach-extension, force-amplifying, and surface-contact tasks compared with object-only VLA/planning, affordance-only grasping, and mobile base-arm optimization.
